Learning Robotics With TWC Classrooms

At TWC Classrooms, we offer interactive and hands-on robotics education for students from ages 4 to 20. Our curriculum is designed to teach the fundamentals of robotics, programming, and engineering in an engaging and practical way. We believe in empowering students to become creators, not just consumers of technology.

Levels to Start:

We have designed our robotics to suit different age groups and experience levels.

Intro to Robotics (Ages 4-6): Fun and engaging activities to introduce young students to basic robotics concepts like movement, structure, and basic programming.
Basic Machines & Mechanisms (Ages 7-10): Students will learn how simple machines work and begin to build their own basic robots using hands-on tools and parts.
Intermediate Robotics (Ages 10-13): A deeper dive into building and programming more complex robots. This course teaches logic, sensors, and motor control.
Advanced Robotics & Programming (Ages 13-20): Focuses on advanced robotics, including autonomous systems, artificial intelligence (AI), and project-based learning. Students will build robots capable of solving real-world problems.

Why Learn Robotics?

Robotics is the future of technology. By learning robotics, students gain valuable skills that go beyond the classroom, such as:

🔧
Problem-Solving: Building and programming robots teaches students how to break down complex problems and solve them step-by-step.
🧠
Critical Thinking: Robotics encourages logical thinking and creative problem-solving, helping students approach challenges from different angles.
🤝
Collaboration: Working in teams to design and program robots fosters collaboration and teamwork, essential skills in today’s world.
🔬
STEM Skills: Robotics combines science, technology, engineering, and mathematics, preparing students for future careers in these fields.
